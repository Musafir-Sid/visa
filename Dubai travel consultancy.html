Types of Dubai visa

There are various types of Dubai tourist visas available based on their validity and entry types. Before you apply for your Dubai visa, you need to know which visa is the right one for your according to the purpose of your visit and duration of stay. The Dubai visa fees will also vary based on the type of visa you opt for.

Here is the list of Dubai visas available to help you choose. -

• Transit visa - There are 2 types of transit visas, a 48-hour transit visa and a 96-hour transit visa. You can apply for this visa if you are travelling to another country and have a stopover in Dubai. It is a single-entry visa.

• 14 days Tourist Visa - This visa is recommended for those who plan on visiting the UAE for a short duration, business meetings, conferences, events, tourism, transit stay etc. The 14-day Dubai visa is valid for 60 days but the duration of stay cannot exceed 14 days. It is a single-entry visa.

• 30 days Tourist Visa - This type of visa is recommended for those who wish to visit family and friends in the UAE or explore the emirates. There is a single entry 30 days visa as well as a multiple entry 30 days Dubai visa. Both visas are valid for 60 days with a stay duration not exceeding 30 days. However, with a multiple-entry visa, you can enter and exit Dubai multiple times within the duration of 60 days.

• 60 days / 90 days Tourist Visa - This visa is ideal if you wish to stay in the UAE for a long time period. You can apply for either a single entry 60-day visa or a multiple entry 60 days visa. This visa entitles you to stay for up to 60 days or less in the UAE.

Reasons for Dubai visa rejection

While applying for a visa it is important for the traveller to make sure they go through the eligibility criteria for a Dubai visa. They also need to check the documents required and provide the right information.

Even if you may have submitted the right documents, there is a possibility that your Dubai visa gets rejected. Here are some of the reasons for the rejection of Dubai visa.

  • If you haven't submitted proper documents.
  • If there are mistakes in your visa application form.
  • If your passport holds an unskilled profession like a housekeeper, farmer, labourer etc.
  • If you are a female below the age of 23, travelling alone.
  • If you have previous criminal or misconduct records.
  • If you did not utilize the Dubai visa you previously applied for.

The final decision on Dubai visa approval or rejection is in the hands of Dubai Immigration.
